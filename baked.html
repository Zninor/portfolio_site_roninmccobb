FairShare

Baked is an app that shows users how to make Cannabases (cooking oils infused with THC) and how to add them to recipes to create delicious, high end edibles like desserts, snacks, and savory dinners.

Making food infused with marijuana (edibles) can be intimidating. If you don't know what you're doing, you can mess up the ratios and consume more THC than you'd like, inducing a bad reaction for yourself and others, or add too little and feel nothing at all.

How can this become an easier and safer process that more people would feel comfortable trying?

I began by interviewing others who would be interested in such a product, to see what features they'd like to see. Here are some highlights from those interviews.

After interviewing several friends to see what they would want out of such an app, I created these personas to represent their various wants, needs, and barriers to making edibles.

After analyzing the various user needs I discovered through interviews, I condensed them all into a single Minimum Viable Product that Baked must achieve: The product must show users a variety of edible recipes and calculate dosages based on user preference, as well as calculate and save cannabases that users make to alleviate ratio errors.

Retrospective

How did I decide on the MVP?

Through interviews, I learned that expanding the concept of the edible beyond sweets would make the whole process more appealing. Furthermore, it was absolutely essential that recipes could be applicable to any user, and that meant making dosages adjustable.

What insights did I gain from testing?

It became clear that when making an app designed for novices, defining key terms such as cannabase, edible, THC, was necessary to avoid confusion. The user tests showed that when these terms were not clearly separated from one another, that users quickly got lost or thought one thing meant the other. This set the stage for the UI portion of this project, where I focused on making elements more clear to the user.

What roadblocks did I encounter?

Placing the UI element for editing a recipe's THC content was crucial. It had to fit into the standard online recipe page, but still be present enough that users were drawn to it before diving into the cooking. This important step in the process was difficult to fit into the user flow without completely taking the user out of the flow, and went through many iterations throughout the project.
